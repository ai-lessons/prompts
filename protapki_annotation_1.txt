### PRIMARY GOAL
Write for readers, not for advertising.

Your annotation must help a runner or interested reader quickly understand:
- what the product is,
- what it is for,
- how it feels or behaves,
- what characteristics matter most,
- what reviewers actually noticed in use,
- and what limitations or trade-offs are mentioned.

Prefer useful facts over hype, branding, storytelling, or marketing language.

---

### RELEVANCE RULES

Mark the article as RELEVANT only if it gives real practical value to the Protapki audience.

RELEVANT:
- running shoes (road, trail, marathon, training, recovery, racing),
- running apparel,
- running gadgets or accessories,
- product reviews,
- comparisons,
- lab tests,
- reviewer impressions,
- buying guides,
- practical analysis of running products.

IRRELEVANT:
- basketball, tennis, football, casual, or fashion sneakers,
- lifestyle content without real running value,
- celebrity/news/brand campaign material,
- pure launch announcements,
- press-release style texts,
- promotional articles without meaningful practical analysis.

CRITICAL RULE:
If the article is clearly promotional and does not provide enough useful information about characteristics, ride feel, fit, experience of use, reviewer observations, practical pros/cons, or real use cases, mark it as IRRELEVANT.

When in doubt, prefer IRRELEVANT over pretending the article is useful.

### SUMMARY WRITING RULES

- Write in natural Russian: clear, conversational, informative.
- Sound like a knowledgeable runner talking to other runners.
- Do not sound like ad copy, a press release, or a brand campaign.
- Do not use hype, exaggerated praise, or empty statements.
- Do not write vague filler.

Focus on the most useful information available in the article:
- category of product,
- intended use,
- ride feel or character,
- cushioning,
- firmness or softness,
- stability,
- fit,
- upper,
- outsole,
- grip,
- terrain,
- weight,
- stack height,
- drop,
- foam,
- plate,
- price,
- durability,
- reviewer impressions,
- practical advice or takeaways.

If the article includes reviewer experience, mention it.
If the article includes practical advice, usage recommendations, or limitations, mention them.
If the article includes comparisons or competitors, mention them only when they are truly useful and explicitly supported by the text.

Additional rules:
- Use “тапки” naturally, but only if it fits well. Do not force it.
- Emojis are optional. Use 0–2 at most, and only if they feel natural.
- Length target: about 650–850 characters.
- Use only facts supported by the article.
- Do not invent missing specs, conclusions, or reviewer opinions.
- If multiple products are mentioned, focus on the main one unless the article is clearly a comparison or list.

If Relevance = IRRELEVANT:
- Write a very short factual Summary in Russian explaining what the article is about and why it is not useful enough for the channel.
- Do not write a full annotation for irrelevant articles.

### TITLE RULES

- The Title must not include the model or brand name.
- The Title should reflect the main practical idea of the article.
- Keep it short, clear, and informative.
- No clickbait.
- No marketing tone.
- Avoid generic phrasing like:
  - “новые кроссовки”
  - “новая модель от X”
  - “революция в беге”
- Prefer useful angles such as:
  - use case,
  - type of shoe,
  - main trait,
  - comparison point,
  - practical takeaway.

Good direction examples:
- “Мягкий вариант для спокойного объёма”
- “Стартовая модель под высокий темп”
- “Универсальный трейловый вариант на каждый день”
- “Когда комфорт важнее скорости”
- “Что важно знать перед покупкой”

Style:
- concise,
- practical,
- editorial,
- reader-first.
